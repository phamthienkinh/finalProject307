TYPES OF COFFEE

1. Arabica (Coffea arabica): This is the most popular type of coffee bean, accounting for about 60% of the world's coffee production. Arabica coffee is grown in cooler climates, typically between 1,200 and 2,200 meters above sea level. It has a mild, sweet flavor with notes of chocolate, fruit, and nuts. 2. Robusta (Coffea canephora): This is the second most popular type of coffee bean, accounting for about 40% of the world's coffee production. Robusta coffee is grown in warmer climates, typically below 1,200 meters above sea level. It has a strong, bitter flavor with notes of earth and grass. Robusta beans contain more caffeine than arabica beans, making them a popular choice for espresso blends and instant coffee.

BEAN VARIETIES

1. Arabica (Coffea arabica): - Bourbon: A mutation of the Typica variety, Bourbon coffee is known for its complex flavor profile with notes of chocolate, fruit, and floral undertones. - Caturra: A natural mutation of the Bourbon variety, Caturra coffee is prized for its bright acidity and citrus notes. - Typica: The original Arabica variety, Typica coffee has a mild, sweet flavor with hints of chocolate and nutty undertones. 2. Robusta (Coffea canephora): - Conillon: A rare variety of Robusta coffee, Conillon has a milder flavor profile than other Robusta beans with notes of chocolate and nuts. - Nganda: A hybrid of Robusta and Liberica coffee, Nganda has a unique flavor profile with hints of spice and tobacco. - Kopi Luwak: Not technically a bean variety, Kopi Luwak is made from partially digested Robusta coffee cherries that have been eaten and excreted by the Asian palm civet. The resulting coffee has a rich, complex flavor with notes of chocolate and fruit.

COFFEE & PASTRY

1. Espresso and Cannoli: The strong, bold flavor of espresso pairs perfectly with the sweet, creamy filling of a cannoli. 2. Cappuccino and Croissant: The frothy, creamy texture of a cappuccino complements the flaky, buttery layers of a croissant. 3. Latte and Danish Pastry: The smooth, creamy texture of a latte balances the sweetness of a Danish pastry, which can be quite rich. 4. Americano and Blueberry Muffin: The slightly bitter flavor of an Americano is a great contrast to the sweetness of a blueberry muffin. 5. Mocha and Chocolate Croissant: The rich, chocolatey flavor of a mocha is the perfect match for a chocolate croissant, which is filled with chocolate ganache or spread. 6. Flat White and Almond Croissant: The nutty flavor of almond in the croissant pairs well with the smooth, creamy texture of a flat white. 7. Iced Coffee and Carrot Cake: The sweetness of carrot cake is balanced by the refreshing coolness of iced coffee.
