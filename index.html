PREP TECHNIQUES Coffee

1. Measure your coffee: Use a scale to measure out the correct amount of coffee beans for the number of cups you're making. A general rule of thumb is to use 1-2 grams of coffee per 1 ml of water.

2. Grind your coffee: Use a burr grinder to grind your coffee beans to the appropriate size for your brewing method. For drip coffee, use a medium grind; for espresso, use a fine grind; and for French press, use a coarse grind.

3. Preheat your equipment: Before brewing, preheat your coffee maker or pour-over equipment with hot water to ensure that it's at the correct temperature (around 200Â°F) when you add the coffee.

4. Add your coffee: Add your measured and ground coffee to the brew basket or filter, making sure to distribute it evenly.

5. Add water: Slowly pour water over the coffee, making sure to saturate all of the grounds evenly.

6. Brew your coffee: Allow the coffee to brew according to your preferred method (drip, pour-over, French press, etc.).

7. Enjoy your coffee: Once the coffee is finished brewing, pour it into a mug and enjoy! Don't forget to clean your equipment thoroughly after each use to prevent buildup and ensure that your next cup is just as delicious.

COFFE FOR HEALTH

It's important to note that the health benefits of coffee may vary based on factors such as the type of coffee consumed (e.g., regular vs decaf), the brewing method used, and individual factors such as genetics and lifestyle habits. Additionally, excessive coffee consumption (more than 400 mg of caffeine per day) may lead to negative health effects, such as increased anxiety and disrupted sleep patterns.
